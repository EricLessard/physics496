Lorentz Transformation

Similar to our Galilean Transformation, let us consider two reference frames, S and S' with S' moving relative to S with constant velocity v.

Consistent with the postulates of Einstein from Lesson2, the Galilean transformations must be altered. The (correct) equations were proposed by Lorentz, and recognized by Einstein. These aforementioned equations are the Lorentz transformations, and they describe the changes observed in measurable space and time. These alterations (as one can check) take more appreciable effects for larger values of v -- specifically when v is a significant fraction of c.

This transformation can be simplified to a multiplicative factor dubbed the Lorentz Factor.


You'll notice that with v << c that γ ≈ 1. This reduces our Lorentz Transformations to our classic Galilean Transformation for speeds much less than the speed of light. Which would be everything we encounter on a day to day basis.

In the following two lessons, applications of the Lorentz Factor will be seen when the moving frame S' approaches the speed of light
