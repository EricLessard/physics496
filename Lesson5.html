In the theory of relativity, time dilation is a measurable difference in the elapsed time between two events as measured by two observers. These observers are moving relative to each other, the causal mechanism of the dilation of time.

Similarly to length contraction as seen in Lesson4, the time dilation effect can be measured as a multiple between the time elapsed by the stationary observer and the relativistic factor γ. The realtionship can be seen below:


A perfect example of this scenario is the twin paradox.

A "gedanken" involving identical twins, one of whom makes a journey into space in a high-speed rocket and returns home to find that the twin who remained on Earth has aged more (i.e, the person travelling faster measured less time than the one still in a reference frame)

gedanken = german for thought experiment
